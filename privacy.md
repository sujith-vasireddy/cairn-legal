# Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 21, 2026

Cairn ("the app," "we," "us") is a private outdoor journal for 
iPhone. This policy describes what data the app collects, how 
it's used, and what choices you have.

## What stays on your phone

Everything you create in Cairn is stored on your phone. This 
includes:

- Your routes, recorded when you start an outing
- Your cairns (the places you mark), including any photos and 
  notes you attach
- Your outing history
- Your display name and preferences

We don't upload any of this to a server. If you delete the app 
or use the "Delete all my data" option in Settings, this data 
is permanently removed.

## Location

The app uses your location only when you've started an outing. 
When you end an outing, location tracking stops. Cairn does not 
track your location in the background at any other time.

Location data is stored on your phone and is never transmitted 
to our servers.

## Photos

When you attach a photo to a cairn, the photo is copied to the 
app's private storage area on your phone. We never upload 
photos to a server.

When you share a cairn, the photo is passed to whichever app 
you choose (Messages, WhatsApp, Instagram, etc.) through iOS's 
standard share sheet. Cairn does not control what that app 
does with the photo afterward.

## Contacts

Cairn does not access or store your contacts. When you share a 
cairn or set up an emergency contact, iOS shows its own native 
contact picker. Only the single contact you choose is passed 
back to Cairn, and only their name and phone number are stored.

## Analytics

Cairn uses PostHog to understand which features are actually 
used. We send anonymous event data like "outing started" or 
"cairn dropped" — never your photos, notes, locations, name, 
or contacts.

Each device gets a random ID that's not tied to any personal 
information. We cannot use this data to identify you.

You can turn off analytics at any time in Settings → Privacy → 
"Help improve Cairn."

## Messages you send

When you share a cairn or an outing through Cairn, the app 
prepares a message in your phone's Messages app (or another 
app you choose). You tap Send yourself. We never send messages 
on your behalf.

The overdue safety net (if you've enabled it) prepares one 
text message to the contact you chose, if you haven't ended 
your outing by your expected return time. You still tap Send 
— Cairn does not send it automatically.

## Third parties

Cairn uses the following third-party services:

- **PostHog** — anonymous usage analytics (opt-out available 
  in Settings)
- **Apple Maps / Google Maps** — map tile rendering (standard 
  iOS map services)
- **Apple CoreLocation** — for GPS during outings
- **Apple Photos** — for the photo picker when you attach a 
  photo to a cairn

We do not sell or share your data with any other third party.

## Children

Cairn is not designed for children under 13 and we do not 
knowingly collect data from them.

## Changes to this policy

If this policy changes materially, we'll update the "Last 
updated" date and note the changes in an app update.
